# ReMynd Assessment Operating System (RAOS)
## Psychometrician Training Manual

---

## 1. Your Role

As a **Psychometrician**, you are responsible for the scoring and reporting phases of every assessment case. Your two primary responsibilities are:

1. **Scoring** — Calculating and reviewing domain scores from completed assessment instruments.
2. **Report approval** — Reviewing and formally approving the assessment report before it is released to the family.

Your access spans from the **Setup** phase through to **Debrief**. You have no involvement in the early intake or pre-commitment stages.

---

## 2. Logging In

1. Go to the RAOS system URL provided by your administrator.
2. Enter your email address and password.
3. You will land on the **Dashboard**.

> If you have forgotten your password, contact your administrator.

---

## 3. Your Navigation Menu

After logging in, you will see the following menu items in the left sidebar:

| Menu Item | What it does |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Dashboard** | Overview of active cases and your pending actions |
| **Cases** | List of all cases you are assigned to — click any case to open it |
| **Assessment Tools** | Library of standardised assessment instruments used across cases |

> You will **not** see Inquiries or Team Management — those are restricted to administrators and invigilators.

---

## 4. The Case Lifecycle — Your Involvement

Cases move through the following phases. Your involvement begins at **Setup** and continues through **Debrief**:

```
Pre-Commitment → Intake → Setup → Forms → Assessment → Scoring → Report → Final Review → Debrief → Complete
```

| Phase | Your Role |
|-------|-----------|
| Setup | Case is being configured — visible to you but no action required |
| Forms | Respondents completing rating scales — visible to you, no action required |
| Assessment | Live session with student — visible to you, no action required |
| **Scoring** | Your primary action — calculate and review domain scores |
| **Report** | Your primary action — collaborate on report, mark as final to approve |
| Final Review | Admin prepares delivery — visible to you |
| Debrief | Report released to family — visible to you |

> Cases in Pre-Commitment and Intake phases are not visible to your account.

---

## 5. Opening a Case

1. Click **Cases** in the sidebar.
2. Find the student's case (sorted by most recently updated).
3. Click the row to open the case detail page.

The top of the case page shows:
- The **phase stepper** — which step the case is currently on
- **Student Details** — name, school, grade, language, parent contact
- The active phase panel for your current task

---

## 6. The Scoring Phase — Your Primary Responsibility

When a case reaches the **Scoring** phase, your job is to calculate and verify domain scores.

### Viewing Scores

1. Open the case and click the **Scoring** tab (if not already on it).
2. You will see all completed assessment instruments grouped by tool and respondent type.
3. Each tool shows domain-level scores and a normalised total.

### Calculating Scores

1. On the Scoring page, click **Recalculate Scores**.
2. RAOS automatically computes domain scores from the submitted responses.
3. Review the output for each respondent type — parent, teacher, invigilator, self-report, etc.
4. Check for any **high discrepancy flags**, which indicate that two respondents scored the same domain very differently. These should be noted in the report.

> **Important:** If a respondent's form is incomplete, their scores will not appear. Contact the administrator if expected responses are missing.

---

## 7. The Report Phase — Your Primary Responsibility

When the case advances to the **Report** phase, your job is to collaborate on the written report and formally approve it before it can be released.

### Accessing the Report Draft

1. Open the case and click the **Report** tab.
2. If a Google Doc has been linked by the administrator, you will see an **Open Google Doc** button — click this to open the shared working document.
3. You and the administrator can edit the document simultaneously in real time.

> The Google Doc link is permanent for this case — you can return to it at any time during the report phase.

### Reviewing the Report

Work through the report document with the administrator:
- Verify all domain scores are accurately reflected.
- Confirm clinical interpretations match the score data.
- Check that recommendations are appropriate and clearly written.
- Ensure the student's name, school, and other details are correct throughout.

### Approving the Report — "Mark as Final"

Once you are satisfied that the report is complete and accurate:

1. On the **Report** tab, click **Mark as Final**.
2. Your approval is recorded in the system (shown as a green checkmark).
3. The administrator must also click **Mark as Final** on their side.
4. **Only when both approvals are recorded** can the report be attached and sent to the family.

> If revisions are needed after you have marked as final, the administrator can withdraw approval which will also clear your approval — you will need to re-confirm after changes are made.

---

## 8. Deploying Assessment Instruments

As a psychometrician, you can deploy assessment tools to a case from the case detail page.

1. Open the case during the **Setup**, **Forms**, or **Assessment** phase.
2. In the **Assignments** section, click **Add Tool**.
3. Select the appropriate instrument from the library.
4. Assign it to the correct respondent type (parent, teacher, self, etc.).
5. Click **Deploy**.

> You cannot deploy **intake forms** — those are restricted to the intake phase and managed by the administrator.

---

## 9. Assessment Tools Library

The **Assessment Tools** section in the sidebar gives you a read-only view of all instruments available in the system. Here you can:
- Review which domains each tool covers
- Check which respondent types each instrument is designed for
- See the structure of tools before deploying them to a case

---

## 10. What You Cannot Do

To protect case integrity and the delivery process, your account has the following restrictions:

| Action | Available to you? |
|--------|------------------|
| Advance or step back phases | No |
| Create a new case | No |
| Edit student or parent details | No |
| Upload the final PDF report | No |
| Send report links or emails to the family | No |
| Manage team members | No |
| View or action new inquiries | No |
| Override the administrator's approval | No |

---

## 11. Tips for Accurate Scoring and Reporting

- **Check all respondents are complete** before recalculating — a missing form means missing data in the score summary.
- **Flag discrepancies early** — if you notice a high discrepancy between parent and teacher scores, raise it with the administrator before the report is written so it can be addressed in the clinical narrative.
- **Use the Google Doc comment feature** — rather than making unilateral changes, use comments in the shared doc to flag areas for discussion with the administrator.
- **Do not approve prematurely** — only click "Mark as Final" when you are fully satisfied. Withdrawing approval after the fact requires the administrator to re-run the approval process.
- **Check the student's language** — the parent email and portal will be delivered in the student's set language (English or Chinese). Ensure the report language matches.

---

## 12. Getting Help

If you encounter any issues:

- **Login problems** → Contact your administrator to reset your password.
- **Case not visible** → The case may still be in Intake; it will appear once it advances to Setup.
- **Score looks incorrect** → Check whether all respondent forms are fully completed; incomplete forms are excluded from scoring.
- **Google Doc not linked** → The administrator needs to link the working document; contact them to do so.
- **Any other system issue** → Contact your ReMynd system administrator.
