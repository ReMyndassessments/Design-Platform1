ReMynd Academic Resilience & Persistence Scale (RARPS)
Version 2.0 – Operational Deployment Standard
I. PURPOSE (FINAL POSITIONING)
RARPS is a non-diagnostic, multi-informant screening tool designed to assess:
Academic persistence
Effort regulation
Motivation and goal orientation
Response to challenge and failure
✔ RARPS DOES:
Identify barriers to academic engagement
Detect resilience strengths and vulnerabilities
Support Tier 2 intervention planning
Contribute to MTSS / RTI decision-making
Feed AI-generated student support profiles
❌ RARPS DOES NOT:
Diagnose learning disorders
Replace psychoeducational assessment
Provide cognitive or achievement scores

IV. STRUCTURE
Total Items: 50
Subdomains: 6
Items per Subdomain: 8–9
V. REVISED ITEM SET (OPTIMIZED WORDING)
🔷 1. Task Persistence
Continues working even when tasks are difficult
Completes assignments despite challenge
Maintains focus during extended tasks
Stays engaged when effort is required
Returns to tasks after distraction
Persists without immediate reward
Completes longer-term assignments
Sustains attention during independent work
🔷 2. Effort Regulation
Adjusts effort based on task difficulty
Recognizes when increased effort is needed
Avoids rushing through work
Maintains consistent effort throughout tasks
Applies appropriate effort when capable
Manages workload effectively
Balances speed and accuracy
Maintains productivity across the day
🔷 3. Academic Self-Concept
Believes they can succeed academically
Shows confidence in learning ability
Feels capable when learning new material
Regains confidence after mistakes
Avoids negative self-labeling during difficulty
Understands that effort leads to success
Maintains a positive academic identity
Believes they can improve with effort
🔷 4. Help-Seeking Behavior
Asks for help when needed
Seeks clarification when confused
Uses available supports independently
Accepts help when offered
Does not avoid asking for support
Uses feedback to improve performance
Seeks peer support appropriately
Requests help before frustration escalates
🔷 5. Response to Failure
Recovers after academic setbacks
Remains engaged after making mistakes
Uses errors as opportunities to learn
Continues effort after low performance
Accepts correction without disengaging
Tries alternative strategies after difficulty
Maintains effort after negative feedback
Stays engaged after being corrected
🔷 6. Motivation & Goal Orientation
Sets personal academic goals
Monitors progress toward goals
Shows intrinsic motivation to learn
Completes work without needing pressure
Values learning over simply finishing tasks
Goes beyond minimum requirements
Maintains longer-term academic goals
Connects effort to future outcomes
Shows curiosity in learning
Stays engaged without immediate rewards
VI. SCORING SYSTEM
🔢 Subscale Scores
Sum of items per subdomain
OR mean score (recommended for AI system)
📊 Functional Bands (Per Subscale)
Score Range	Interpretation
0–8	Minimal concern
9–16	Mild concern
17–24	Moderate concern
25–32	Elevated concern
🧠 IMPORTANT (SYSTEM ADJUSTMENT)
Because items are scored 0–4:
👉 Use MEAN SCORES for consistency across tools
Mean Score	Interpretation
0.0–1.0	Strength
1.1–2.0	Mild concern
2.1–3.0	Moderate concern
3.1–4.0	Elevated concern
VII. AI INTERPRETATION LOGIC
🚩 Flag Conditions
Trigger intervention consideration when:
≥ 2 subdomains = Moderate
OR ≥ 1 subdomain = Elevated
OR High discrepancy between respondents
🔍 Pattern Detection Examples
Pattern	Interpretation
Low persistence + high failure sensitivity	Avoidance profile
Good effort + low self-concept	Confidence deficit
Poor help-seeking + high struggle	Hidden risk
High motivation + low execution	Executive function issue
